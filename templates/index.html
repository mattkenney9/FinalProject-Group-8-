    Frequently Asked Questioins

    Everything you need to know so you can navigate The Ocho like a pro.

    • What is IC50 and what is the significance?

      Half-maximal inhibitory concentration (IC50) is the most widely used and informative measure of a drug's efficacy. It indicates how much drug is needed to inhibit a biological process by half, thus providing a measure of potency of an antagonist drug in pharmacological research.

    • How is technology helping to advance the Biomedical field?

      One key benefit of technology is that it allows scientists to gain mechanistic insight into human biology and human response to drugs; and it has the potential to be used in many fields, including drug discovery, food and chemical research, as well as precision and regenerative medicine.

    • How did your team come up with this project idea?

      Nick has a passion for Bioinformatics and more specifically Biomedical data. The team quickly rallied behind the idea, saw an opportunity to learn something new, create something fun, and build cool things.

    • What is BioInformatics?

      Bioinformatics is a subdiscipline of biology and computer science concerned with the acquisition, storage, analysis, and dissemination of biological data, most often DNA and amino acid sequences. Bioinformatics uses computer programs for a variety of applications, including determining gene and protein functions, establishing evolutionary relationships, and predicting the three-dimensional shapes of proteins.

    • Where did you get the BioInformatic data?

      We gathered our data primarily from the ChEMBL database. The ChEMBL database brings together chemical, bioactivity, and genomic data to aid the translation of genomic information into effective new drugs.
